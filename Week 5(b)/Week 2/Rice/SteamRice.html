Steam Rice

Steamed rice, also known as plain boiled rice, is a staple in Pakistani cuisine. It is a simple yet essential component of many meals, serving as a versatile base for various curries, stews, and other dishes. To prepare steamed rice, long-grain rice, such as Basmati rice, is commonly used. The rice is thoroughly rinsed with water to remove excess starch, ensuring that the cooked grains remain separate and fluffy. The rinsed rice is then placed in a pot with the appropriate amount of water, usually using a ratio of 1:2 (one part rice to two parts water). The pot is covered with a tight-fitting lid and the rice is cooked on low heat until all the water is absorbed and the rice is tender. It is important to resist the temptation to stir the rice during cooking, as this can lead to a sticky texture. Once the rice is cooked, it is left covered for a few minutes to allow the steam to finish cooking and to let the grains separate further. This resting period helps to achieve perfectly cooked, fluffy rice. Steamed rice is a versatile accompaniment to many Pakistani dishes. It can be served alongside curries, grilled meats, lentils, or any other flavorful main course. Its neutral taste allows it to complement the rich flavors of the accompanying dishes. Steamed rice is a beloved part of Pakistani cuisine, offering a simple and nourishing carbohydrate base that adds balance and satisfaction to a variety of meals.
