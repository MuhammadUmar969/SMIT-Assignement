Daal Mash

Daal mash, also known as white lentil dal, is a flavorful and creamy lentil dish commonly enjoyed in South Asian cuisine. It is made from white lentils, also known as white urad dal or split urad dal, which have a mild and nutty taste. To prepare daal mash, the white lentils are soaked in water for a few hours to soften them. Then, they are cooked in a pot with water, turmeric, and salt until they become tender and creamy. The lentils are simmered on low heat until they reach a smooth and velvety consistency. In a separate pan, a tempering mixture is prepared by heating oil or ghee and adding cumin seeds, minced garlic, chopped onions, and optional spices like ginger, green chilies, and garam masala. Once the tempering mixture is fragrant and the onions are caramelized, it is added to the cooked lentils, enhancing the flavors of the dish. Daal mash is often garnished with fresh coriander leaves and served with rice, naan bread, or roti. It is a comforting and nutritious dish, rich in protein and fiber. The creamy texture and delicate flavor of daal mash make it a popular choice for vegetarian meals or as a side dish to complement other main courses. The simplicity and versatility of daal mash allow for variations in spice levels and additional ingredients based on personal preferences. It is a beloved dish in South Asian households and is enjoyed for its comforting taste and nourishing qualities.
