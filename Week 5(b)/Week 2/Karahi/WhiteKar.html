White Karahi

White karahi is a popular Pakistani dish known for its creamy and flavorful taste. It is called "white" karahi because of its creamy white appearance. The dish is typically made with boneless chicken or mutton cooked in a blend of spices, yogurt, cream, and butter. The flavors of garlic, ginger, and green chilies add a delightful kick to the dish. White karahi is enjoyed with naan or rice and is a favorite among those who prefer a milder yet rich and creamy curry option. The preparation of white karahi involves slow cooking the meat with the spices and other ingredients until it becomes tender and absorbs the flavors. The creamy and smooth texture of the dish comes from the addition of yogurt and cream, giving it a luxurious and indulgent taste. White karahi is a popular choice for special occasions and gatherings, offering a delicious twist to traditional Pakistani cuisine with its creamy and flavorful profile.
