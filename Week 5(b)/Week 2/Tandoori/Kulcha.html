Lahori Kulcha

Lahori kulcha is a popular street food and a specialty of Lahore, a city in Pakistan. It is a type of stuffed bread that is known for its unique flavor and texture. Lahori kulcha is made from a dough consisting of all-purpose flour, yeast, yogurt, salt, and a touch of sugar. The dough is allowed to rise, and then small portions are taken and flattened into oval or round shapes. The special feature of Lahori kulcha lies in its filling. It is typically stuffed with a spicy and tangy mixture made from boiled and mashed potatoes, onions, green chilies, cilantro, and a blend of aromatic spices. This filling is then sealed inside the dough and baked in a tandoor or on a hot griddle until it turns golden brown and develops a slightly crispy exterior. Lahori kulcha is often served with a side of chole (chickpea curry) and is enjoyed as a hearty and flavorful breakfast or snack. The combination of the soft and fluffy bread with the spicy and savory filling creates a mouthwatering culinary experience that is loved by locals and visitors alike in Lahore.
