Finger Fish

Finger fish, also known as fish fingers or fish strips, is a popular seafood appetizer enjoyed in Pakistan. It consists of boneless fish fillets that are marinated, coated in a seasoned batter, and deep-fried to a crispy golden brown. To prepare finger fish, boneless fish fillets, such as tilapia or cod, are commonly used. The fish fillets are cut into thin strips or bite-sized pieces for easy handling and quick cooking. The fish strips are then marinated with a mixture of spices, including red chili powder, turmeric, cumin, coriander, and salt. The marinade helps to infuse the fish with flavor and tenderize it. Next, the marinated fish pieces are coated with a batter made from a combination of flour, cornstarch, eggs, and various spices. The batter gives the fish a crispy and crunchy texture when fried. The fish strips are carefully dropped into hot oil and deep-fried until they turn golden brown and become crispy on the outside. It is important to fry them in small batches to ensure even cooking and prevent overcrowding in the fryer. Once cooked, the finger fish is typically served hot and garnished with fresh coriander leaves. It is commonly enjoyed as an appetizer or snack, served with a side of tangy tamarind chutney or mint yogurt sauce. The crispy and flavorful finger fish is a popular choice for seafood lovers in Pakistan. Its bite-sized portions and delightful taste make it a favorite appetizer or snack during gatherings, parties, or as a side dish with a main course.
