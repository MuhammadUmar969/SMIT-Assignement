Dhakka Fish

In Dhakka Fish, fish fillets, such as Rui (Rohu) or Katla, are commonly used. The fish is marinated with a mixture of spices, including turmeric, red chili powder, cumin, coriander, ginger, garlic, and mustard oil. The marination helps to infuse the fish with a rich and aromatic flavor. To prepare the curry, a sauce is made by frying onions until golden brown and adding a combination of spices, such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder. Tomatoes and green chilies are then added to the sauce, along with water or fish stock to create a flavorful base. The marinated fish fillets are gently placed into the simmering sauce and cooked until they are tender and fully cooked through. The fish absorbs the flavors of the spices and the tanginess from the tomatoes, creating a delicious and well-rounded dish. Dhakka Fish is typically served hot with steamed rice or naan bread. It is a popular choice among fish enthusiasts, as it combines the natural taste of the fish with a spicy and tangy curry that is characteristic of the Dhaka region. Please note that while Dhakka Fish may have influences and variations in neighboring regions, the specific recipe and preparation can vary among households and individual preferences.
