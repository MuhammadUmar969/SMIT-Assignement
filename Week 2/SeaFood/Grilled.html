Grilled Fish

Grilled fish is a popular and delicious dish enjoyed in Pakistan, particularly in coastal regions where fresh seafood is abundant. It is a simple yet flavorful preparation that allows the natural taste of the fish to shine. To prepare grilled fish, a whole fish, such as pomfret, trout, or snapper, is commonly used. The fish is first cleaned and scaled, and then marinated with a mixture of spices, including red chili powder, turmeric, cumin, coriander, and salt. Lemon juice or yogurt may also be added to the marinade for added tanginess and tenderness. The marinated fish is then placed on a grill or barbecue, either directly on the grates or in a grilling basket, and cooked over medium heat. The fish is typically grilled until it is cooked through, with a crispy and slightly charred exterior. During grilling, the fish is often basted with oil or melted butter to keep it moist and to enhance the flavors. The basting also helps to prevent the fish from sticking to the grill. Grilled fish is often served hot off the grill, accompanied by a side of mint chutney or tamarind chutney for added flavor. It is typically enjoyed with naan bread or rice, and may be served with a fresh salad or vegetable side dish. The smoky and charred flavors of grilled fish, along with the blend of spices, create a mouthwatering experience. It is a popular choice for seafood lovers in Pakistan, offering a delightful combination of simplicity and deliciousness.
